Assistant Professor · Department of Cognitive Linguistics · Faculty of English

I am a cognitive linguist working with various types of data, including language, gesture, as well as visual and literary narratives. I publish on conceptual metaphor theory and its multimodal, multilevel aspects, trying to understand how our minds make sense of the world around us.

Conceptual metaphor theory Gesture studies Multimodal cognition Narrative analysis

Research Profile

Current & Expanding Directions

My work focuses on how conceptual metaphors operate across modalities and levels of representation, from image schemas and gesture to discourse and narrative structure. I am particularly interested in how multimodal cues shape affective meaning and social positioning.

Expanding research directions:

  • Literary semantics and cognitive stylistics
  • Gesture-based emotional cues in narrative
  • Multimodal meaning-making in game studies
  • Multilevel models of conceptual metaphor and metonymy
